What Is an IRA?

An IRA is a type of retirement account that is offered by many employers and investment brokers. An IRA is a tax-deferred account that allows people to invest in stocks, bonds, and other assets. IRAs are a great way to save for retirement because they allow you to both save and grow your money at the same time. There are a few different types of IRAs: Traditional IRAs - A traditional IRA is an account that you can contribute money to on a tax-deductible basis. A traditional IRA allows you to invest in stocks, ETFs, bonds, and other types of assets. Roth IRAs - A Roth IRA is an account that you can contribute to without being taxed on the money. Roth IRAs offer you more flexibility than traditional IRAs because you don’t have to pay taxes on the money you put into the account. Roth IRAs are a great way to invest in stocks and other types of assets if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in the future.
