What is an ETF?

ETFs are investment funds that track a specific market segment. They are similar to mutual funds in that they are owned by a large number of investors and can be traded on an exchange. ETFs are often preferred by investors who want to invest in a particular market but don’t want to buy individual stocks or other assets. ETFs are usually cheaper than owning a large number of individual securities because there is less risk for the investors.ETFs are also very liquid, which means you can buy and sell them easily throughout the day. This is especially helpful for investors who want to rebalance their portfolio often.An ETF’s value is based on the performance of the underlying assets it holds. If the price of those assets increases, the ETF’s value also increases. If the price of the underlying assets drops, the ETF’s value will drop as well.
